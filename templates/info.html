Project Highlight

Dataset is obtained from Analytics Vidhya where is used for multilabel classification competition for Topic Modeling for Research Articles. The purpose is finding relevant articles as easy as possible from large online archives of scientific articles. Tagging or topic modelling provides a way to give token of identification to research articles which facilitates recommendation and search process.

What unique about this search engine?

Traditionally, we use comparsion/matching of keywords entered by users against tables in database. The challenge with traiditional search is that the more keywords have to be searched in each and every tables in database, the more complex scripting will become and it is a nighmare for maintenace and performance optimization. Moreover, the approach ignores the intent of message, the meanings of words under different context. There is no mathematical ranking order for return search output. Programmer either have to sort the output by alphabetcial or numerical or by date. For example, examine below two sentences.

  • A thief robbed the bank last night.
  • He went to bank to see sunset.

There is a common word 'bank'; however, it has different meaning under different context. To overcome this problem as well as ranking, there is a state-of-the-art model called Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT), a latest model widely used in natural language processing developed by Google researchers. BERT is a pre-training model trianed on Books Corpus with 800M words and English Wikipedia with 2,500M words. This greatly helps to reduce training time on model building while maintaining high performance on document classification, question and answering, text generation, etc. The recommendation search engine is used BERT for query search and recommendation on similar articles.
